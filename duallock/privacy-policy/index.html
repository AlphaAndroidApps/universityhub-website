Who we are

Dual Lock is an Android app-lock utility that helps you protect user-selected apps with separate Normal and Critical access rules. This policy applies to the Dual Lock Android app, including the lock-screen flow, setup flow, and related on-device protection features.

Privacy contact: admin@universityhub.co.in

What the app does

Dual Lock monitors when apps that you chose to protect come to the foreground on your device. When that happens, Dual Lock shows its own lock screen over the protected app and asks for the appropriate PIN or, when enabled, a biometric check through Android.

Dual Lock is designed to operate primarily on-device. It does not require an account, and the current app code does not include advertising, analytics, or cloud sync features.

Data we access or store

Depending on how you configure Dual Lock, the app may access or store the following categories of information:

  • Protected app selections: the package names of the apps you choose to protect and whether you marked them as Normal or Critical.
  • Launchable app catalog: the package names, labels, and icons of apps that expose standard launcher entries on your device, so Dual Lock can let you choose which apps to protect.
  • App-usage events: Android usage-access events that indicate when apps come to the foreground, so Dual Lock can decide when to show the lock screen.
  • Lock configuration: settings such as session timeouts, whether to ask every time, whether biometrics are enabled, whether the critical PIN may unlock normal apps, and related protection preferences.
  • PIN processing data: the PINs you create are processed on-device and stored locally as salted hash values rather than raw PIN text.
  • Biometric result signals: if you enable biometric unlock, Dual Lock receives only the success, failure, availability, or cancellation result returned by Android's biometric APIs. Dual Lock does not receive or store your fingerprint image or biometric template.
  • Temporary runtime lock state: short-lived local records used to track active or recent unlock state, lock cooldowns after repeated failed attempts, pending lock targets, and recent manual-stop recovery signals.
  • Consent and setup state: whether you accepted the in-app sensitive data disclosure and whether required permissions are currently available.

How data is used

  • To show you the list of launchable apps available for protection.
  • To detect when a protected app opens and display the correct lock screen at the right time.
  • To enforce your chosen Normal and Critical protection rules, session windows, and re-lock behavior.
  • To verify PIN attempts locally and to trigger lockout or cooldown behavior after repeated incorrect entries.
  • To ask Android for biometric verification when you enable that option for Normal or Critical apps.
  • To recover protection monitoring after reboots, app updates, or user-initiated restarts where Android allows it.
  • To show an ongoing foreground notification while protection monitoring is active or to warn you when required permissions or monitoring are unavailable.

Data sharing and transfers

Dual Lock's current implementation is designed to keep its protection data on your device. The app does not use an account system, does not sync your protected-app configuration to a remote server, does not show ads, and does not include analytics SDKs in the current codebase.

  • No sale of personal data: we do not sell protected-app information, PIN data, or usage-access data.
  • No routine third-party sharing: the current app flow does not transmit your protected-app list, PIN hashes, or usage-access history to our servers.
  • Android platform services only: when you use biometric unlock, the verification itself is handled by Android's biometric framework on your device.
  • Legal or safety requests: if we ever receive a valid legal request related to support or abuse handling, we would respond only as required by applicable law.

Permissions and device access

Dual Lock may request the following Android permissions or special access, depending on the feature you enable:

  • Usage Access (`PACKAGE_USAGE_STATS`): used to detect when a protected app becomes active so Dual Lock can show the lock screen.
  • Display over other apps (`SYSTEM_ALERT_WINDOW`): used so Android can allow the lock screen to appear above the protected app you opened.
  • Biometric access (`USE_BIOMETRIC`): used only when you enable biometric unlock in the app.
  • Foreground service: used to keep protection monitoring active while showing an ongoing user-visible notification.
  • Boot and app-update restart handling (`RECEIVE_BOOT_COMPLETED` and package update events): used to re-arm monitoring after reboot or app update where Android allows it.

If you deny or revoke these permissions, some or all app-lock features may stop working until you enable them again.

Retention and deletion

Dual Lock keeps its protection configuration locally on your device for as long as needed to provide the feature or until you change, clear, or remove it.

  • Protected-app selections, preferences, and local PIN hash material stay on your device until you change them, clear app data, or uninstall the app.
  • Temporary unlock state, pending lock targets, and cooldown state are local runtime records that expire automatically or are cleared as protection state changes.
  • You can remove most stored Dual Lock data by clearing the app's storage in Android settings or uninstalling the app.

Security

Dual Lock uses local-only protection logic, stores PIN material as salted hash values instead of raw PIN text, and relies on Android system APIs for permissions, overlays, and biometric verification. However, no software or device environment can guarantee absolute security, and some data remains subject to the protections of the device and operating system where the app is installed.

Children's privacy

Dual Lock is a general utility app and is not specifically directed to children under 13. If you believe a child has used the app in a way that raises a privacy concern, contact us and we will review the request.
